CONTEXT

What is BOP in this Program?

📌 Definition

Base of the Pyramid (BOP) refers to the global population earning under USD 3,000/year — roughly 4 billion people. In the KOICA IBS framework, BOP youth are the primary training beneficiaries: those excluded from conventional higher education who gain pathways out of poverty through practical technical skills.

🇵🇭 Philippines Context

~43% of the Philippine population falls in the BOP bracket. Target trainees are aged 18–30, from low-income households in urban-fringe and rural areas, who cannot afford private technical training (avg. cost: USD 800–2,500/course) but have access to TESDA public institutions.

🎯 IBS Objective

IEG equipment + KOICA ODA funding creates a zero-cost or subsidized training pathway. Trainees gain TESDA EIM NC II/III certification, enter manufacturing employment, and achieve a projected +40–60% income increase. This is the core social value proposition for KOICA evaluation.
