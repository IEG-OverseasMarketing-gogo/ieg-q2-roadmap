Co-development Opportunity Matrix — Three Tiers

Ranked collaboration opportunities by feasibility, speed to market, and strategic value for IEG.

01
Tier 1 · Immediate · High Feasibility

IEG × PSDC FutureTech Academy

PLC / Automation Equipment Supply + DKM L4 IAT Co-curriculum

  • IEG delivers PCS Hands-on Equipment (Siemens + Mitsubishi) to PSDC's existing DKM L4 IAT lab — slot-in hardware upgrade to an accredited programme.
  • Co-develop a "Semiconductor-Grade PLC" add-on unit within PSDC's IAT DKM L4 framework — adds Siemens SMSCP and SECS/GEM content not currently available in Malaysia.
  • PSDC registers IEG M-2 + M-3 content under its HRD Corp provider number — IEG accesses SBL-Khas without waiting for own registration.
  • PSDC's 200+ MNC member base (Intel, Infineon, Micron) becomes IEG's instant customer pipeline for in-house training.
  • Revenue split model: IEG supplies hardware + curriculum IP; PSDC operates training and claims HRD Corp levy — shared fee structure.
02
Tier 2 · Medium-term · Strategic Value

IEG × PSDC × Kesuma ASK (Tripartite)

Semiconductor Smart Factory Technologist Programme

  • Propose a new "Semiconductor Smart Factory Technologist" national qualification — combining PSDC's DKM L4 IAT with IEG's IMOBA AMR + Digital Twin equipment and ASK's AI-in-fab context.
  • Target a new SKMC (Malaysian Modular Skills Certificate) — enabling stackable micro-credentials rather than a full diploma, lowering barrier to entry for Intel ATM upskilling.
  • IEG M-5 (IMOBA + EasyFASGEM + Digital Twin) is the anchor hardware. PSDC provides JPK accreditation. ASK contributes AI design-to-fab context lectures.
  • Eligible for Budget 2026's RM 6.8B TVET allocation as a national semiconductor priority programme — strong government grant potential beyond HRD Corp SBL-Khas.
  • Positions IEG as the sole equipment supplier in a government-backed national semiconductor TVET programme — the strongest IPO narrative asset available in Malaysia.
03
Tier 3 · Long-term · IEG × ASK Bilateral

IEG × Kesuma ASK (JMTI)

Process Equipment for Chip Design Visualisation & ATM Bridge

  • ASK trains chip designers — but chip designers need to understand how their designs are manufactured, packaged and tested. IEG's 8-process testbed and AR/XR VR content bridges this design-to-manufacturing knowledge gap.
  • Propose an "ATM Bridge Module" for ASK: IEG M-4 content (8-process testbed + VR) delivered at JMTI to chip design students — 2-day immersive ATM literacy course.
  • Leverage IEG's AR/XR Semiconductor Process VR Learning Content as ASK's low-cost entry point — no physical equipment needed, VR licence model.
  • ASK is a government body (KESUMA/JTM) — MOU with JMTI opens ADTEC network (33 campuses) for IEG's AR/XR VR content licensing nationally.
  • Long-term: ASK's AI design graduates become the talent pipeline for companies buying IEG equipment — virtuous ecosystem loop.
Section 4

Gap Analysis & Collaboration Strategy — Where IEG Leads vs. Where It Follows

Strategic positioning guide for IEG across the PSDC and ASK partnership landscape.

✅ Areas Where IEG Leads — Unique Contribution

🔬 Semiconductor Process Simulation (M-4)

Neither PSDC nor ASK currently operates an 8-process semiconductor testbed. IEG's Testbed of 8 Major Semiconductor Processes and Photo Process Testbed are unavailable anywhere else in Malaysia's accredited TVET landscape.

→ IEG should assert content ownership and negotiate equipment-supply MOU with PSDC as host.
🤖 AMR & Digital Twin for Semiconductor (M-5)

IMOBA Wafer Cassette Transfer Robot and EasyCluster Digital Twin are not available in any Penang TVET institution. PSDC's robotics content is general industrial — not semiconductor-specific.

→ IEG proposes a joint "Semiconductor AMR Technician" SKMC micro-credential with PSDC.
📡 SECS/GEM FAB Automation Standard

No Malaysian TVET institution currently teaches SECS/GEM. IEG's EasyFASGEM is the only training-grade SECS/GEM equipment in the region.

→ Exclusive: position IEG as the SECS/GEM TVET standard-setter in Malaysia in partnership with SEMI Malaysia.
🥽 AR/XR VR Semiconductor Learning Content

IEG's 12-inch wafer VR content covers the full ATM process in an immersive environment. This gives ASK a high-quality entry point into hands-on process literacy for chip designers.

→ Licence VR content to JMTI / ASK under a per-seat annual model. Low capital, high margin.

⚠ Areas Where IEG Must Leverage Partners

📜 JPK / HRD Corp Accreditation

IEG is a foreign equipment supplier with no Malaysian legal entity or HRD Corp registration. All SBL-Khas claims must flow through a Malaysian registered training provider. PSDC is the ideal host.

→ IEG must structure a revenue-sharing agreement where PSDC registers the programme and IEG receives equipment fees + curriculum royalties.
🔭 Live Wafer & ATE Practical Experience

PSDC has MNC relationships (Intel, Micron, Infineon) but no wafer-handling facilities. Neither does IEG. Live wafer practicals remain the critical gap in M-4 that neither party can fill alone.

→ Negotiate a three-way arrangement: IEG (testbed theory) + PSDC (host + accreditation) + Micron Malaysia / PSDC's Intel link (live wafer practicals). PSDC is the logical broker.
🏅 Industry Certification Links (ISO 14001, QC080000)

Intel JDs explicitly require ISO 14001 / QC080000 certification — neither IEG nor PSDC currently delivers a certification-linked pathway for these standards in TVET context.

→ SIRIM QAS International (now in PSDC Block 3 since July 2025) is the natural co-development partner. PSDC introductions recommended.
🌐 Chip Design / IC Upstream Context (ASK territory)

IEG's equipment addresses ATM (downstream assembly and test). Chip design, EDA software, and IC verification are ASK's domain. IEG cannot and should not compete here.

→ Respect the boundary. Position IEG M-4/M-5 as the "ATM literacy layer" for ASK's chip design graduates — complementary, not competitive.

Recommended MOU Roadmap — Sequenced Action

Three-step MOU sequencing — H2 2026 to Q2 2027

01
Step 01 · Immediate — via Sean / FCoEE

PSDC FutureTech Academy — Equipment MOU

IEG supplies PCS Hands-on Equipment (Siemens + Mitsubishi) + SECS/GEM Interface to PSDC's IAT lab. PSDC registers M-2 + M-3 content under HRD Corp. Revenue sharing: IEG 60% hardware / PSDC 40% training ops. Target sign: Q4 2026.

Step 02 · H1 2027 — tripartite

PSDC + ASK (JMTI) — Curriculum Co-development MOU

Jointly develop "Semiconductor Smart Factory Technologist" SKMC micro-credential. IEG: M-5 hardware (IMOBA + Digital Twin). PSDC: JPK accreditation + HRD Corp. ASK: AI-in-fab context module. Target submission to JPK: Q2 2027.

03
Step 03 · H2 2027 — bilateral

JMTI / ASK — VR Content Licence Agreement

IEG licences AR/XR Semiconductor Process VR Learning Content to JMTI for ASK's chip design students. Annual per-seat licence model (USD 200–300/seat). Opens ADTEC national network (33 campuses) for IEG VR content distribution.

Key prerequisite for Step 01: Confirm PSDC's willingness to accept a foreign equipment partner and revenue-share model. PSDC has precedent — SIRIM QAS International occupies Block 3 as a resident partner since July 2025. Sean Lim (Galaxy Global) and FCoEE should facilitate the first introduction to PSDC's training director. Ask Sean: "Do you have an existing relationship with PSDC or JMTI?" — the answer determines whether the June 30 Penang visit should include a PSDC call.

Note on ASK timing: The Kesuma Semiconductor Academy was announced 30 April 2026 and its curriculum is still being developed. This is the optimal window to influence what IEG equipment and content gets embedded from the start — approaching ASK now (before curriculum lock-in) is significantly more valuable than doing so after.
